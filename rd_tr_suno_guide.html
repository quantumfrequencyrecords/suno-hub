Russell Dickerson's sonic signature: He slyly incorporates modern R&B rhythms into bright, tuneful mainstream country-pop — warm acoustic guitar layered over modern production, emotive tenor vocals, and lyrics rooted in domestic romantic love. His songs are almost exclusively about his wife Kailey and their life together. The production style pairs organic country instruments (acoustic guitar, pedal steel) with contemporary pop sheen. His recent era infuses a country-soul foundation with lush synth, distorted 80s rock crunch, and thumping modern beats.

Thomas Rhett's sonic signature: He combines country, pop, R&B and even hip-hop — modern pop, R&B, and hip-hop elements are woven throughout, while his Georgia twang signals reverence for country's oldest traditions. His songs fall into two production buckets: warm polished country-pop ballads ("Die a Happy Man," "Unforgettable") and unorthodox genre-bending experiments ("Crash and Burn," "Life Changes"). His fans truly love a story, and an honest one. Unlike Dickerson who writes almost exclusively about his wife, Rhett writes across a much broader range: nostalgia, biographical life chapters, attraction, devotion, and self-reflection.

However, there are three practical workarounds that let us achieve the same goal — building Suno prompts from your actual liked songs library:

01

Paste Your Liked Songs List

Export or copy your Spotify liked songs (or just type a batch of 10–20 at a time) and paste them into a message to me. I'll research each one, extract the sonic DNA, and generate Style + Lyric Generation prompts for every song. You can do this in batches — just say "here are 15 more songs."

How to get your list: Spotify → Your Library → Liked Songs → select all → copy track names, or use a free tool like Exportify (exportify.net) to download as a CSV.
02

Tell Me Your Playlists

Instead of individual songs, tell me the names of your Spotify playlists and roughly what artists are in them. I can build a genre/mood profile from that and generate a master prompt set covering your overall taste — a universal style prompt and lyric brief that captures your vibe across everything you like.

Just say something like: "My main playlist has Morgan Wallen, Zach Bryan, Russell Dickerson, Thomas Rhett, Dan + Shay, and Kelsea Ballerini" — that's enough for me to work with.
03

Suno's Own Spotify Feature

Suno has a "Personalize" feature in some versions that can connect to Spotify and analyze your listening history to influence its generation style. Check your Suno account settings under Profile → Personalize or Taste Profile. This is separate from what I can do — it's built into Suno directly.

If you're on Suno Pro or Premier, this feature may already be available to you. It won't write prompts for you, but it'll bias Suno's generations toward your preferred sounds.
